What we do

Topaz builds software that improves itself while it runs. Most programs are frozen the moment they ship — they do exactly what they were written to do until a person rewrites them. A Topaz program treats its own behavior as adjustable and tunes it continuously from live signal, the way a model trains, except it happens in production and on its own.

Why now

Until now, software that adapts meant a research team, a training pipeline, and months of work — so almost nothing you use actually does it. Topaz makes adaptive software something you write in one language and ship, instead of a project only a lab can afford.

That is the shift: continuous learning becomes a default property of software, not a specialist feature.

The first product

A landing page that optimizes itself. A/B testing needs roughly 27,000 visitors to call a single winner — traffic most sites never get, so their pages just sit there. A Topaz page learns from the traffic it already has.

Here is how. The page is compiled into a differentiable program, so every visual choice — layout, spacing, color, emphasis — is a continuous parameter rather than a fixed value. Visitor behavior (scroll depth, hovers, dwell time) is a dense signal, far richer than convert-or-not, and the page follows it the way a neural network follows a gradient. It does not pick the best of a few human-written variants — it searches a continuous space of designs and settles on ones no one drew by hand.

The stack underneath

The product runs on a stack we built from the ground up. Each layer is shipped and working today.

  • Sutra, our language. Logical operations over vector spaces compile at build time into differentiable tensor graphs, so an entire program is one continuous, trainable function. Published on PyPI as sutra-dev (v0.7.0), with a NeurIPS 2026 submission.
  • An operating system written in Sutra, GPU-native and neuro-symbolic, where the whole running system is a single inspectable network. Working prototype with a terminal, calculator, and click-driven GUI.
  • Self-optimizing pages, the first product — the same stack at ground level, where the page itself learns from every visitor.
